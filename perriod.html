The Three Period Lesson

What is the Three Period Lesson in the First Plane?

  • First Period: Naming
    • The guide introduces an object or concept using precise language. For example, they might say, “This is a circle,” while holding up a circle.
  • Second Period: Recognizing
    • The guide helps the student connect the name to the object by asking them to interact with it. They might say, “Can you put the circle on your head?” to engage the student playfully.
  • Third Period: Remembering
    • In this final stage, the student recalls the object's name independently. The guide asks, “What is this?” while holding up the circle, prompting the student to name it

What is the Three Period Lesson in the Second Plane?

  • During this stage, students transition from concrete thinking to abstract reasoning. The Three-Period Lesson is adapted to match their developing reasoning mind:
    • First Period: Naming
      • Instead of saying, “This is a circle,” as in the first plane, the guide provides a detailed explanation such as, “Today, we are going to explore different types of angles. Here is a right angle.”
    • Second Period: Recognizing
      • Instead of presenting objects directly, the guide encourages exploration, asking, “I wonder if we can find any right angles around the room?”
    • Third Period: Remembering
      • Instead of presenting objects directly, the guide encourages exploration, asking, “I wonder if we can find any right angles around the room?”

Additional Elements of the Three Period Lesson

  • Key Lesson
    • The main introduction to a concept, often presented through storytelling, experiments, or demonstrations.
  • Follow-Up or Extension Work
    • Activities that deepen understanding, such as:
      • Research projects
      • Creative work (art, storytelling)
      • Experiments
      • Discussions
  • Assessment
    • Away to know if the students have a full understaning
      • Observation
      • Presentations
      • Journal entries
      • Collaborative discussions that demonstrate comprehension
