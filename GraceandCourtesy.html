Grace and Courtesy

Grace and courtesy teach students how to behave thoughtfully and respectfully toward others and themselves. These lessons form the foundation of the classroom, helping to create social harmony and peace. They cover manners and communication skills, fostering a caring and supportive community.

Core Elements

  • Manners and Politeness:
    • Saying “please,” “thank you,” “excuse me,” and using polite greetings.
  • Respect for Others:
    • Listening when someone else is speaking, waiting for a turn, and using gentle language
  • Care for the Environment:
    • Handling materials carefully, cleaning up, and respecting shared spaces.
  • Self-Control and Emotional Regulation:
    • Practicing patience, managing frustration calmly, and expressing feelings appropriately.

How to Teach Grace and Courtesy

  • Model Behavior:
    • Adults demonstrate respectful actions consistently.
  • Role-Playing and Practice:
    • Children practice through guided activities and real-life situations.
  • Positive Reinforcement:
    • Notice and celebrate moments when children use grace and courtesy naturally.
  • Gentle Correction:
    • Redirect without shaming when social norms aren’t followed.

Why It Matters

  • Grace and courtesy help children feel safe and valued. It lays the groundwork for strong social skills, empathy, and cooperation, skills that benefit children long after they leave the classroom. These lessons encourage children to think about how their actions affect others and how they can contribute to a peaceful community. They learn to listen attentively, speak kindly, and respect personal space. By practicing these skills daily, children develop habits that support a positive classroom environment and prepare them for healthy relationships in the wider world.
